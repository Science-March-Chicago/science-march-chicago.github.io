Diversity, Accessibility and Inclusivity

We support a science that is representative and ethical, a science that is better. These are issues that cannot afford silence and require diligent, deliberate action and communication.

  • We ascribe to the Diversity Principles established by the national March for Science movement.
  • We ascribe to the Anti-Harassment policy established by the national March for Science movement.
  • We ascribe to the Statement on Peaceful Assembly and Nonviolence established by the national March for Science movement.

As we finalize our own diversity statements, representative of the needs in Chicago, we will update this page with those set of principles.
We recognize that each of these efforts require more than words but actions. And as we publish a statement communicating each of these, the philosophies, principles and associated behaviors must be dynamic enough to respond to feedback, experience, and context. If you have any ideas, feedback or questions about our approach in this area, please email diversity@sciencemarchchicago.org, and help push forward these efforts.
