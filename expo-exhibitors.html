March for Science Expo

Exhibitor Information

Call for Exhibitors | Timeline | Fee Information | Rules | FAQ

The March for Science Chicago Expo is an opportunity for Chicagoans to take their next step in supporting science in Chicago; whether it’s getting involved in Chicago’s scientific community, supporting scientific organizations, or learning about the science of the world around them.

We’re inviting individual scientists (graduated or students), organizations, and nonprofit groups to join us at our Expo to engage Chicagoans in science-based experiences. Exhibitors at the Expo can promote their role in Chicago’s scientific community or their causes to a motivated, interested audience of 40,000 Chicagoans. We hope you’ll consider joining us at the Expo as an investment in Chicago, its citizens, and its science.


Call for Exhibitors

We are looking for organizations and individuals who:

  • Directly work in the sciences by researching, teaching, and/or studying
  • Support the scientific community by promoting, fundraising, and/or advocating for scientists/research
  • Act based on scientific facts
  • Engage in the scientific method
We permit the following activities at the Expo:

  • Science demonstrations or activities
  • Displaying/presenting research posters or science projects
  • Recruiting people for volunteer or other positions at your organization
  • Giving away merchandise
  • Promoting your organization and/or its upcoming events
  • Asking for donations (proof of 501(c)(3) status required; please see disclaimer on the form here)
However, please note that we are unable to accommodate the following:

  • Speakers
  • Performances
  • Large scale screenings; you may bring your own computer to show video
  • Activities which require electricity
March for Science Chicago does not and shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, creed, gender, gender expression, age, national origin (ancestry), disability, marital status, sexual orientation, or military status, in any of its activities or operations. These activities include, but are not limited to the selection of volunteers and booths for the March and the Expo. We are committed to providing an inclusive and welcoming environment for all members of our staff, volunteers, and Expo exhibitors, as well as for all those marching with us to support the sciences in Chicago and our nation as a whole.

This is a “Leave No Trace” event. Any Expo exhibitor that engages in activities including but not limited to, littering, abandoning property, damaging tables, chairs or other Expo materials, throwing confetti, dropping flyers, or damaging Field Museum property in any manner may be fined and/or barred from participation in future events with March for Science Chicago.


Timeline

We highly recommend that you apply and pay your fees as soon as possible. See Fee Information for detail regarding the application versus exhibitor fee.

  • All proposals will be reviewed, and exhibitors will be carefully selected to ensure a diverse experience.

  • You will receive instructions on how to pay the exhibitor fee upon notification of your proposal’s approval.
  • Following payment of the exhibitor fee, MFS Chicago will provide a preliminary table assignment and instructions prior to the event.

3. Expo: April 22, 2017

Rules

  • Any science demonstrations must be approved by MFS Chicago.
  • This is a family-friendly event with a high volume of participants. Any demonstrations that cause loud explosions, messes, flashing lights, release of gasses or fumes, or any other disruption that could cause discomfort or disturbance must be reviewed by March for Science Chicago and may be rejected due to safety concerns. Any such disruptions not approved by March for Science Chicago that Expo exhibitors conduct at this event may result in ejection from participation, and may result in the group or individual being barred from participating in future March for Science events.
  • Expo registration is non-transferable. If your application is approved, you may not give, sell, donate, or otherwise disseminate your spot to another organization.
  • No selling or giving away food, beverages, or alcohol.
  • No selling merchandise.


FAQ

When does my organization need to be at the Expo location?

Once your application has been approved and your fee paid, we will provide set-up/tear-down instructions. Please note that at least one representative from your organization must be present at your table from set-up to tear-down. Plan accordingly if you have representatives who would like to participate in the rally/march.

What happens to the Expo if it rains?

We will continue to hold the Expo if the rain is manageable. If rain is forecasted, we highly suggest bringing your own 10’ x 10’ canopy. If there is a severe storm forecast, we will cancel the Expo and notify you via email. Information will also be available via social media and our website. The application and exhibitor fees are non-refundable.

Who should I contact if I have any other questions?

Please email expo@sciencemarchschicago.org and include “Exhibitor: “ in the subject line before the title of your email.
