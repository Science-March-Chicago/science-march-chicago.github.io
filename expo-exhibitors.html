The March for Science Chicago Expo is an opportunity for Chicagoans to take their next step in supporting science in Chicago. Our goal is to bridge the public and the science it serves through volunteering opportunities with Chicago’s scientific community, supporting scientific organizations, or learning more about the science of the world around them and how to support them.

We’re inviting individual scientists (graduated or students), organizations, and nonprofit groups to join us at our Expo to engage Chicagoans in science-based experiences and how to become involved through support or other opportunities. Exhibitors at the Expo are encouraged to discuss how their science serves Chicago’s scientific community or their causes to a motivated, interested audience of 40,000 march participants.

This is opportunity to join a conversation and talk directly about science and how it serves our community.


Call for Exhibitors

We are looking for organizations and individuals who:

  • Directly work in the sciences by researching, teaching, and/or studying
  • Support the scientific community by promoting, fundraising, and/or advocating for scientists/research
  • Act based on scientific facts
  • Engage in the scientific method
  • Develop action plans based on scientific facts and engage in the scientific method
We permit the following activities at the Expo:

  • Conversation with march participants about how the science you use serves Chicago
  • Science demonstrations or activities, within reason and without power
  • Displaying/presenting research posters or science projects
  • Recruiting people for volunteer or other positions at your organization
  • Giving away merchandise
  • Promoting your organization and/or its upcoming events
However, please note that we are unable to accommodate the following:

  • Speakers
  • Performances
  • Large scale screenings; you may bring your own computer to show video, but power is not provided
  • Other activities which require electricity

March for Science Chicago does not and shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, creed, gender, gender expression, age, national origin (ancestry), disability, marital status, sexual orientation, or military status, in any of its activities or operations. These activities include, but are not limited to the selection of volunteers and booths for the March and the Expo. We are committed to providing an inclusive and welcoming environment for all members of our staff, volunteers, and Expo exhibitors, as well as for all those marching with us to support the sciences in Chicago and our nation as a whole.

This is a “Leave No Trace” event. FAQ

What is the purpose of the Expo?

After the march, we want Chicagoans to be able to take their next step in supporting the science community and get involved in scientific efforts:

  • Research happening in Chicago that directly impacts our community
  • Learn about participating in Citizen Science efforts
  • Sign up to volunteer with organizations to directly impact our community
  • Find out more about how you can help guide science in The Science City
Will I be able to park at the Expo?

Parking at the Expo, Field Museum, and Soldier Field will not be available. If you need to park your car, we suggest parking at the Millennium Parking Garage.


When does my organization need to be at the Expo location?

Once your proposal has been approved and costs paid, we will provide set-up/tear-down instructions. Please note that at least one representative from your organization must be present at your table from set-up to tear-down. Plan accordingly if you have representatives who would like to participate in the rally/march.

What happens to the Expo if it rains?

We will continue to hold the Expo if the rain is manageable. If rain is forecasted, we highly suggest bringing your own 10’ x 10’ canopy. If there is a severe storm forecast, we will cancel the Expo and notify you via email. Information will also be available via social media and our website. The application and exhibitor costs are non-refundable.

Who should I contact if I have any other questions?

Please email expo@sciencemarchschicago.org and include “Exhibitor: “ in the subject line before the title of your email.
